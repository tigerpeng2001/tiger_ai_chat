# Theology Analysis: The "As" of Forgiveness (Matthew 6:12)

This analysis explores the linguistic roots of [Matthew 6:12](https://www.bible.com/bible/114/MAT.6.NKJV?parallel=2692) and the theological perspective that forgiving others is fundamentally an act of releasing oneself from a burden.

## 1. Linguistic Breakdown: *Hōs* ($\omega\varsigma$)
In the original Greek script, the word translated as "as" is **$\omega\varsigma$ (hōs)**. 

* **Grammatical Function:** It is a comparative particle meaning "in the same manner" or "just as." 
* **The Wenli Context:** In the [文理和合譯本](https://www.bible.com/users/tigerpeng2001/reading-plans/22357-the-lords-prayer-explained/subscription/1271966947/day/5/segment/2), this is rendered as **如 (rú)** in the phrase **“如我免人”** (*rú wǒ miǎn rén*), which reinforces the idea of a "like-for-like" pattern.
* **Logical Link:** The grammar suggests a **standard of measure**. We are asking God to apply the same principle of mercy to us that we participate in with others.

## 2. The Theology of "The Released Burden"
Forgiveness is not merely a favor to the offender, but a necessity for the victim's own spiritual health.

* **Forgiveness as Self-Release:** To forgive is to put down a weight. If bitterness is a burden held by the self, then releasing that bitterness is, in effect, "forgiving" one's own soul from the duty of carrying a grudge.
* **The Mirror Effect:** By practicing the "release" of others (**免人**), we open the internal capacity to receive the "release" of our own debts (**免負**) from God.

## 3. Comparison: Forgive vs. Forget
Within the context of [The Lord's Prayer](https://www.bible.com/bible/114/MAT.6.NKJV?parallel=2692), these two concepts are distinct:

| Concept | Action | Outcome |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Forgiving** | Active release (*Aphiēmi* - "to send away"). | The "sting" is removed; the memory is no longer a burden. |
| **Forgetting** | Passive bypass or denial. | The weight is ignored but remains; wisdom from the event may be lost. |

## 4. Influential Perspectives
Several theologians share the view that forgiveness is a gift to the self:

* **Lewis B. Smedes:** "To forgive is to set a prisoner free and discover that the prisoner was you."
* **Archbishop Desmond Tutu:** Taught that forgiveness is the "medicine" for the victim's own spirit; bitterness destroys the container it is held in.
* **Dietrich Bonhoeffer:** In [Life Together](https://www.bible.com/users/tigerpeng2001/reading-plans/22357-the-lords-prayer-explained/subscription/1271966947/day/5), he notes that bearing another's sin through forgiveness releases the believer from legalistic slavery.

## 5. Synthesis: Grace vs. Quantity
The feeling of needing "more forgiveness from God than I can offer others" is an honest recognition of our human state.
* **The Source:** We live in the "Already" of God's infinite mercy, but our own ability to forgive is often in the "Not Yet" stage of growth. 
* **The Posture:** The "As" (*hōs*) is not about matching the **volume** of God’s grace, but aligning with its **direction**. We are small streams flowing into the same ocean of mercy.

---
*Note: This analysis focuses on the reflexive nature of grace—when we extend mercy outward, we clear the space for mercy to dwell within us.*
